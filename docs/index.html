ImageStamp

Fix the dates on your scanned photos and videos — quickly and accurately.

📷 What ImageStamp Does

When you scan old photos, the file date gets set to today — not when the photo was actually taken. ImageStamp lets you correct that by stamping the real date directly into the EXIF metadata of your photos and videos.

Once stamped, apps like Apple Photos, Lightroom, and Google Photos will display the correct date automatically.

🚀 How to Use ImageStamp

  1. Drop your files — drag photos, videos, or a folder onto the app, or click Browse Files.
  2. Set the date — use the date picker in the toolbar to choose the correct date.
  3. Review your selection — check or uncheck individual files. Click any file to preview its current metadata.
  4. Stamp — click "Stamp X Files", review the confirmation, and confirm. Done.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Will this affect the image quality?
No. ImageStamp only modifies the metadata (EXIF data) embedded in the file — the actual image pixels are never touched.

Can I undo a stamp?
Yes. Enable "Undo" in the confirm window before stamping. ImageStamp will save a backup copy of each file. After stamping, an "Undo Stamp" button appears in the results view.

What file formats are supported?
JPEG, HEIC, HEIF, PNG, TIFF, RAW (CR2, CR3, NEF, ARW, DNG, ORF, RW2, PEF), MP4, MOV, M4V, AVI, MKV, MTS, M2TS, and 3GP.

Does ImageStamp require an internet connection?
No. Everything runs locally on your Mac. The only exception is the optional GPS location picker, which uses Apple Maps to search for places.

Will the new date show up in Apple Photos?
Yes — after stamping, re-import the photos into Apple Photos (or remove and re-add them) and they will appear under the correct date.

🔐 Privacy Policy

ImageStamp is designed with your privacy in mind. Here's exactly what the app does and doesn't do:

✅
No data collection. ImageStamp does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data, photos, or metadata to any server.
✅
No analytics. The app contains no analytics, tracking, or crash reporting SDKs.
✅
Files stay on your Mac. All processing happens locally. Your photos never leave your device.
✅
File access requires your permission. ImageStamp only accesses files you explicitly select via the file picker or drag-and-drop.
✅
Location is optional. If you use the GPS stamping feature, location search uses Apple's MapKit framework. No location data is stored or shared.
